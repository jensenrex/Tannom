About Us

We are a Cebuano startup with Cebuano founders working towards the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. We plant trees for you with the shared goal of healing Cebu province- our permaculture food and cash crop forest are solutions to the biggest environmental, social and economic issues in the history of our planet. We leverage technology to bring together our contributors, who are our tribe, to regenerate depleted land and help even the remotest communities in our province. We will effect massive positive, sustainable change.

Our team has spent their professional lives in education, permaculture and aquaculture. Tannom's CEO, Jensen, graduated with accounting and law degrees from Australia and completed a MBA at Ateneo de Manila University. In 2012, he organised a $23,000 grant from Rotary International to teach permaculture and provide seedlings to Minganillia mountain barangays and PSP Farm, an organic site for rehab of juveniles in trouble with the law and ex-prisoners. Our passionate permaculture expert, Greg, has done permaculture consultancy, design and training in Australia, Africa and Asia transforming lives, regenerating land, and inspiring self-sufficiency. Our aquaculture expert, Kenneth, graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Aquaculture from James Cook University and has experience in pearls, barramundi and prawns working in Philippines, Indonesia, Papua New Guinea and Australia.

Together, we can take massive steps to solve our county's environmental problems- we need your help to reverse the massive deforestation that has caused landslides, lowered our water table, displaced rural community members, and disrupted ecosystems.

Permaculture

Permaculture, which stands for "permanent agriculture" or "permanent culture," is a holistic approach to designing sustainable and regenerative systems that mimic natural ecosystems. It can play a significant role in addressing the loss of flora and fauna around communities. By incorporating permaculture principles, communities can create resilient and sustainable ecosystems that support the well-being of both flora and fauna. The holistic nature of permaculture design addresses the interconnectedness of all living organisms and their environments, fostering harmony between humans and the natural world. Here's how permaculture principles and practices can help:

  • Biodiversity Enhancement

  • Wildlife Habitat Creation

  • Water Conservation & Management

  • Soil Regeneration & Reduced Chemical Inputs

  • Community Engagement, Education & Awareness

  • Regenerative Agriculture

Biodiversity Enhancement

  • Permaculture emphasizes planting a diverse range of crops, including native species, to create ecosystems that support a variety of plants and animals.
  • Polyculture and companion planting are common permaculture techniques that contribute to biodiversity by creating habitat niches for different species.

Wildlife Habitat Creation

  • Designing landscapes with a variety of plants, including native species, can provide food and habitat for local wildlife.
  • Incorporating features such as ponds, birdhouses, and wildlife corridors can encourage the presence of diverse fauna.

Water Conservation & Management

  • Permaculture designs often include water catchment and storage systems, swales, and other techniques to optimize water use.
  • By managing water effectively, permaculture helps create a more resilient environment for plants and animals, especially during periods of drought.

Soil Regeneration & Reduced Chemical Inputs

  • Permaculture promotes practices such as cover cropping, mulching, and composting to enhance soil fertility and structure.
  • Healthy soils support a diverse range of microorganisms, which in turn contribute to the overall health of the ecosystem.
  • Permaculture advocates for minimizing or eliminating the use of synthetic chemicals and pesticides, which can have harmful effects on both flora and fauna.
  • Organic and natural farming practices are often integrated into permaculture systems, promoting a healthier environment.

Community Engagement, Education & Awareness

  • Permaculture encourages community involvement in the design and maintenance of local ecosystems.
  • Engaging community members in planting native trees, creating community gardens, and restoring natural habitats fosters a sense of responsibility and connection to the local environment.
  • Permaculture principles emphasize the importance of understanding and observing natural patterns and processes.
  • Education programs and workshops on permaculture can raise awareness about the importance of biodiversity and ecological balance.

Regenerative Agriculture

  • Permaculture promotes regenerative agricultural practices that focus on restoring and improving ecosystems.
  • Agroforestry, agroecology, and other sustainable farming methods within permaculture contribute to the conservation of biodiversity.

As the Country Director and overseer of the EMBRACE Project in Nyabihu district, I had the pleasure of working as Greg's supervisor when he was contracted here as a PERMACULTURE Expert trainer.
